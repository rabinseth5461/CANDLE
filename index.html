Crypto 101: Ultimate Knowledge Base

Master the concepts. Every piece of information you need to understand the Crypto world.

The Blockchain

A blockchain is a decentralized, digital public ledger. Instead of a central bank keeping track of money, thousands of computers globally verify and record transactions. It is virtually impossible to hack or alter past records.

Cryptocurrency

Cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency secured by cryptography. It operates on blockchain technology. Unlike fiat money (NPR, USD), it is decentralized and not controlled by a single government.

Bitcoin (BTC)

The first and largest cryptocurrency, often referred to as "Digital Gold." It is primarily used as a store of value.

Ethereum (ETH) & Smart Contracts

The second largest. Ethereum allows developers to build decentralized applications (DApps) and execute automated code called "Smart Contracts."

Altcoins & Memecoins

Any coin that is not Bitcoin is an Altcoin (e.g., Solana, Cardano). Memecoins (like Dogecoin) are driven by community hype and are extremely high-risk.

Stablecoins (USDT, USDC)

Cryptocurrencies pegged to the value of real-world assets like the US Dollar to minimize volatility.

Hot Wallets (Software)

Wallets connected to the internet (e.g., MetaMask, Trust Wallet, Binance). Easy to use for trading, but slightly more vulnerable to online hacks.

Cold Wallets (Hardware)

Physical devices (e.g., Ledger, Trezor) that store your crypto offline. They are the safest way to hold long-term investments.

Spot Trading vs. Futures

Spot: Buying an asset and owning it. You only lose money if the asset goes to zero.
Futures/Leverage: Borrowing money to multiply profits. High risk! You can get "liquidated" (lose everything) if the market moves against you.

Bull vs. Bear Markets

Bull Market: A cycle where prices constantly rise and market sentiment is extremely positive.
Bear Market: A cycle where prices drop heavily and fear dominates the market. Often the best time to accumulate assets.

DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging)

Investing a fixed amount regularly, regardless of the price. This mitigates the risk of buying at the top.

Technical Analysis (TA)

Studying charts and daily "Candles" to predict future price movements. We look at Support (floor price), Resistance (ceiling price), Volume, and indicators like RSI and MACD.

Fundamental Analysis (FA)

Evaluating the actual value of a project. We study the Whitepaper, the founding team, Tokenomics (how coins are distributed), and real-world utility.

  • FOMO: Fear Of Missing Out. Buying at the top because you see others making money.
  • FUD: Fear, Uncertainty, Doubt. Negative news meant to panic sellers.
  • HODL: Hold On for Dear Life. Refusing to sell despite massive price drops.
  • Whale: A person or entity holding a massive amount of crypto, capable of moving market prices.
  • ATH / ATL: All-Time High / All-Time Low.
  • Rekt: Getting wrecked (losing your investment, usually via leverage).
  • Rug Pull: A scam where developers abandon a project and run away with investors' funds.

Trading is 20% strategy and 80% psychology. At CANDLE, we teach the following rules:

  • Always Use Stop-Loss: An automated order to sell if the price drops to a certain level, preventing deeper losses.
  • The 1% Rule: Never risk more than 1-2% of your total portfolio on a single trade.
  • DYOR: Do Your Own Research. Never blindly follow "signals" from unknown sources.
  • Take Profits: Nobody ever went broke taking profits. Don't be too greedy.
